## Elevator pitch

> A private companion to my own MT5 trading journal. Ingests every trade, narrates it against my stated plan, flags style drift, and surfaces the patterns my eyes miss. Not financial advice — a journaling tool with an LLM on the read side.

## What it is

Two services I run privately:

- A NestJS API that ingests MT5 trade history (account, ticket, symbol, side, lots, entry/exit price, p&L, duration) and stores it in Postgres with my journal entries linked by trade-time window.
- A Next.js dashboard where each trade gets a generated narrative, a plan-compliance score, and a "compare to similar past trades" view.

The LLM reads my plan ("trade London open only, max 0.5 lot on EUR pairs, no chasing after a stop"), looks at the trade row, looks at my journal note for that day, and writes 3-4 sentences narrating what happened relative to plan. Pattern drift detection runs across rolling 30-day windows.

## The problem I was solving

I trade for myself on the side. Every retail trader has the same problem: you can see your win-rate, your average R, your drawdowns. You can't see — at any kind of scale — _why_ you drifted from your plan on the days you lost money. The journal is the artefact that should bridge that gap, but reading 200 journal entries to spot a pattern is itself a forty-minute task per week.

An LLM is good at pattern-matching across written notes plus structured trade data. So I let it do the eye-strain part.

## Key decisions

1. **Read-side only.** The LLM never sends a trade. The risk is zero by construction.
2. **Plan stored as prose, not JSON.** "Trade London open only, max 0.5 lot on EUR pairs, no chasing" reads better in a prompt than a structured schema.
3. **Pattern detection on rolling windows.** 7-day and 30-day windows compared to lifetime baseline — flag drift only when both windows confirm.
4. **No public deployment.** It's mine. Repo is private because the prompts encode my personal trading rules.

## Why it's private

The repository is private because the system prompts encode my personal trading methodology, which I'd rather not publish. The work and the architecture are what I'd talk about; the prompts are the IP.
